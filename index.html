About me

Growing up I always knew I wanted to pursue a creative career, art was my favourite subject in school and I didn’t find anything else interesting. However I was bullied when I was 12 and from there I struggled with my self-confidence and self-esteem. When it came to deciding on what I wanted to pursue in the future I made those decision based on what will make my parents proud rather than what am I passionate about. I went to university to study pharmacology and I graduated with a first class honours and then pursued a maters in toxicology a year later.

Before graduating with my masters I gave birth to my son. This is when everything changed, the time I spent at home raising my son was also the time I spent going through a process of self-healing and rebuilding myself. Healing from the trauma I experienced as a child. This completely changed my life, I made a decision to start over and start making decisions for me and my biggest motivation was my son.

I set out on a mission to figure out what I want to do and set myself a deadline to figure it out before my son starts full time school. At first I didn’t know what I was looking for but I kept researching and taking on whatever opportunity I could find, one of the projects I started working on involved using photoshop and from there I began learning how to use it, I started putting my designs on instagram and got some work out of it. For the past two years I have been designing logos, wedding invitations, menus, banners, stickers, personalised mugs and bags. I also developed a passion for calligraphy, with this skill I make handwritten welcome signs for events, and personalised cards. From this experience I realised one of the effective ways to learn is to jump right in and to not be afraid of making mistakes because they are your best lessons. I built a small business around my skills which is growing with time.

Learning to code was a thought I never entertained simply because I just didn’t think I could do it, but with the experience I gained in the last few years I realised I could do anything I set my mind to. I learnt some of the basics of html and css last year and was amazed with what could be done with learning different computer languages. I am super excited to pursue a career in this field and combine it with my creative skills.
